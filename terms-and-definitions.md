# Terms and Definitions
|Term|Definition|
|-|-|
|Blank|A Face with no pips (or the number 0).|
|Boneyard|Where all Tiles start, are shuffled, and drawn from during play.|
|Depot|Central point for the Engine and all player Lines.  Mexican Trains may also start from the Depot.  Also called the Hub or Station.|
|Derail|Laying a Tile that ends in a number with no remaining unplayed Tiles.|
|Double|A Tile where both Faces have the same number of pips.|
|Draw|Take a Tile from the Boneyard either to form a new Hand or because no Tiles in the Hand can be played.|
|Engine|The initial Tile for the current Round.  All Lines start with a Face matching the Engine.|
|Face|Half of a Tile with a number of pips (or an actual number)|
|Game|All the Rounds of a particular playing session.|
|Hand|The Tiles held by a player in the current Round.|
|Line|A Sequence of Tiles laid end to end where the Faces of adjacent Tiles match.  All Lines start by matching the Engine Face number.|
|Marked|A private Train that is temporarily Public allowing any player to lay Tiles on it.|
|Marker|A coin or token used to temporarily change a Private Train to a Public Train.|
|Mexican&nbsp;Train|A Public Train that starts with a tile matching the Engine for the current Round and is always open for any player to lay tiles on it.|
|Private&nbsp;Train|A Line owned by a particular player who has exclusive access to lay Tiles against the Line.  Private Trains may temporarily become Public Trains during the Round.|
|Public&nbsp;Train|All Mexican Trains and all Marked Private trains, if any, that are open to all players to lay tiles on.|
|Round|The play from shuffling the Boneyard, drawing Hands, docking the Engine to the Depot, and laying Tiles until one player empties his Hand.|
|Satisfy&nbsp;a&nbsp;Double|Also Close a Double. Lay another Tile with a Face that matches the Double.|
|Sequence|A set of Tiles that match end to end and are played on a Line in a single turn.|
|Tile|A Domino with two Faces.|
|Uno|A Card game unrelated to Dominos, despite this term being the reason this FAQ exists.|
