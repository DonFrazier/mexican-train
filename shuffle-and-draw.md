# Shuffle and Draw

# Shuffle
Each round starts by turning all Tiles Face down to form the Boneyard and shuffling the Tiles.
Shuffling can be formal or informal.
After the Boneyard is shuffled each player draws a Hand of Tiles for the current Round.
Drawing Hands can be formal or informal.
See [Place Engine](place-engine.md) for additional shuffle considerations.

### Formal Shuffling
In **Round one** the First Player shuffles the Boneyard.  All other players wait until the shuffle is complete before touching any Tiles.

In **subsequent Rounds** the winner of the previous Round shuffles the Boneyard.
All other players wait until the shuffle is complete before touching any Tiles.

### Shuffling
Circulate the entire Boneyard mixing all Tiles.
Avoid knocking Tiles off the table.
Mix all Tiles at least a couple of times. 

## Draw

### Drawing Hands

Without exposing Tiles each player draws the agreed number of Tiles from the Boneyard.
Any Tile exposed by a player must be taken into the Hand for that Round.
If a player draws more Tiles than agreed, unseen Tiles can be returned to the Boneyard.
Having more Tiles increases the chances of matching Tiles on available Trains and can be advantageous.

#### House Rules on Drawing Hands

##### Exposed Tiles
Action if a player exposes Tiles while Drawing:
